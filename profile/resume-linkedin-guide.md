# Resume & LinkedIn Guide for Zahra

How to tailor your resume and LinkedIn for each career pathway you're pursuing. Check this before every application.

---

## LinkedIn Profile — Must-Haves

### Headline
Don't just say "Student at UC Berkeley." Use a formula:

> **Biology Graduate | Marketing (UC Berkeley) | D1 Athlete | Open to Opportunities in Biotech, Education & AI**

### About Section
Hit these points in 3-4 sentences:
- Australian with a biology degree and UC Berkeley marketing certificate
- D1 high jumper — discipline, teamwork, performance under pressure
- Passionate about science education, community engagement, and making science accessible
- **Mention E-3 visa eligibility:** *"Australian citizen eligible for E-3 specialty worker visa — a streamlined process for employers, no lottery required."*

### Experience Section
Make sure these are listed even if informal:
- **UC Berkeley Track & Field** — D1 athlete. Emphasize leadership, time management, competing at the highest level
- **UC Berkeley Extension** — Marketing certificate. List key coursework (digital marketing, analytics, content strategy, etc.)
- **University of Buffalo** — BS Biology. Highlight any research, lab work, tutoring, or TA roles
- **Any teaching/mentoring** — tutoring, coaching younger athletes, church youth group, camp counseling
- **Any marketing projects** — class projects, social media management, event promotion

### Skills to Add
Add all of these to your LinkedIn skills section:
- Marketing Strategy, Content Marketing, Digital Marketing, Social Media Marketing
- Biology, Life Sciences, STEM Education, Science Communication
- Public Speaking, Community Engagement, Event Coordination
- Data Analysis, Google Analytics, Microsoft Office, Adobe Creative Suite
- Teaching, Mentoring, Youth Development, Team Leadership

### Other LinkedIn Tips
- Set "Open to Work" — select Bay Area, and check all relevant job titles
- Add a professional headshot (athletic photos work great if professional)
- Connect with people at every company you're applying to
- Join groups: "Australians in Silicon Valley", "Bay Area Biotech", "Science Education Network"
- Post 1-2x per week about your journey — people love rooting for athletes transitioning to careers

## Resume Checklist — Universal (Every Application)

Every version of your resume should include:

- [ ] **E-3 visa line** at the top: *"Australian citizen — authorized for E-3 specialty worker visa (no lottery, no employer cost)"*
- [ ] **Education** — both degrees listed with dates
- [ ] **Athletics** — UC Berkeley D1 Track & Field, High Jump. Include any records, honors, competitions
- [ ] **Skills section** matching the keywords in the job description
- [ ] **Quantified achievements** — numbers wherever possible (e.g., "trained 15 hours/week while maintaining 3.X GPA")
- [ ] **Tailored summary** — 2 sentences matching the specific role
- [ ] No typos, clean formatting, one page

## Resume Tailoring by Pathway

### 1. Biotech / Pharma Marketing Roles
*Genentech, Gilead, Bio-Rad, Benchling, 23andMe internships and full-time*

**Lead with:**
- Marketing certificate from UC Berkeley (list specific courses)
- Biology degree — emphasize you can understand the science behind the products
- Any content creation experience (even class projects)

**Keywords to include:**
content marketing, product marketing, campaign management, digital marketing, life sciences, biotech, social media, analytics, CMS, email marketing, cross-functional collaboration

**Frame your biology as an advantage:**
> "Unique ability to translate complex scientific concepts into accessible marketing content for both technical and non-technical audiences"

### 2. Science Education / Museum Roles
*Lawrence Hall of Science, Cal Academy, Exploratorium*

**Lead with:**
- Biology degree — this IS the qualification
- Any teaching, tutoring, mentoring, or coaching experience
- Passion for making science accessible

**Keywords to include:**
STEM education, science communication, informal education, curriculum development, youth development, inquiry-based learning, hands-on learning, community engagement, outreach, facilitation

**Frame athletics as relevant:**
> "D1 athlete experienced in mentoring, coaching, and inspiring young people to push beyond perceived limitations"

### 3. Summer Camp / Seasonal Roles
*Lawrence Hall of Science camps, Galileo, iD Tech, YMCA*

**Lead with:**
- Any experience working with kids or teens
- Biology degree (for science camps)
- Athletics — coaching, team leadership, mentoring
- Energy, reliability, enthusiasm

**Keywords to include:**
camp counselor, youth development, STEM activities, team leadership, safety protocols, group facilitation, outdoor education, mentoring

**Keep it simple** — camp applications value personality and reliability over credentials

### 4. AI Company Non-Engineering Roles
*Anthropic, OpenAI, Benchling, Tempus, Recursion*

**Lead with:**
- Marketing certificate — digital marketing, analytics, content
- Interest in AI and technology
- Communication and people skills
- Biology background (especially for AI-bio companies like Benchling, Tempus)

**Keywords to include:**
community management, content marketing, go-to-market, operations, program coordination, cross-functional, stakeholder management, communications

**Frame yourself as a science-literate marketer:**
> "Marketing professional with a scientific background, able to bridge technical teams and external audiences"

### 5. UC Berkeley Staff Roles
*Cal Athletics, Admissions, Student Affairs, Lawrence Hall of Science*

**Lead with:**
- Current Berkeley connection — you're already part of the community
- Athletics experience at Cal
- Marketing certificate from Berkeley Extension
- People skills, student engagement

**Keywords to include:**
student services, program coordination, academic support, event coordination, communications, outreach, admissions, enrollment

**Use your insider knowledge:**
> "Current UC Berkeley student-athlete with deep understanding of the campus community and student experience"

### 6. Graduate School Applications
*USF Sport Management, SFSU, CSUEB, UC Berkeley grad programs*

**This is a CV, not a resume. Include:**
- All education with GPA if strong
- Research experience (any lab work, independent studies)
- Athletic achievements and honors (list them all)
- Teaching/mentoring experience
- Statement of purpose connecting biology + marketing + athletics + future goals
- Letters of recommendation — ask coaches, professors, and supervisors NOW

### 7. Faith-Based / Community Roles
*FCA, InterVarsity, church positions, faith-based nonprofits*

**Lead with:**
- Faith and values — it's appropriate here
- Community engagement and service experience
- D1 athlete background (especially for FCA)
- Teaching and mentoring heart

**Keywords to include:**
ministry, campus outreach, discipleship, community building, youth ministry, program coordination, volunteer management, event planning

### 8. Teaching Credential / K-12 Roles
*California emergency teaching credential, school districts*

**Lead with:**
- Biology degree (biology is a shortage subject in California!)
- Any teaching, tutoring, or mentoring experience
- CBEST and CSET exam status (take these soon if pursuing this path)
- Classroom management, communication, patience

**Keywords to include:**
biology instruction, NGSS standards, differentiated instruction, classroom management, student engagement, formative assessment, lab safety
